# Skill: /polish

Apply the final layer of professional polish to the interface. This skill focuses on aesthetic perfection, rhythmic consistency, and subtle details that elevate a design from "good" to "impeccable."

## When to use
- After a component's core functionality is implemented.
- When a UI feels "stiff" or "basic."
- To ensure consistent application of the "Rule of 8" and brand-specific aesthetics.

## Core Focus Areas
1.  **Optical Alignment**: Adjust positioning for visual balance (e.g., icons in buttons, text in circles).
2.  **Rhythm & Space**: Enforce the Rule of 8. Ensure consistent internal padding and external margins.
3.  **Elevation & Depth**: Use layered box-shadows or subtle gradients to define hierarchy. Avoid "flat" designs where depth adds clarity.
4.  **Edge Treatment**: Refine border-radii (avoid mixing sharp and very round corners inconsistently) and border-widths.
5.  **Micro-interactions**: Enhance hover, active, and focus states with subtle transforms or color shifts.

## Instructions
- Use **OKLCH** for all color adjustments.
- Use **Container Queries** instead of media queries where appropriate for component-level responsiveness.
- Prioritize **logical properties** (`padding-inline`, `margin-block`, etc.).
- Ensure **accessibility** is maintained or improved during the polish phase.
- Simplify where possible. Polishing often means removing unnecessary borders or lines in favor of clear whitespace.
