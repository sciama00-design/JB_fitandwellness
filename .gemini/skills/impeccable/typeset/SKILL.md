# Skill: /typeset

Establish a professional typographic hierarchy and vertical rhythm. This skill focuses on font-family selection, sizing, and spacing for optimal readability.

## When to use
- When the UI feels "text-heavy" or unorganized.
- To improve readability and information hierarchy.
- To standardize font scales and weights.

## Core Focus Areas
1.  **Typographic Scale**: Use a consistent scale (e.g., Major Third: 1.25 ratio). Avoid arbitrary font sizes.
2.  **Vertical Rhythm**: Align typography to the Rule of 8. Line heights should be multiples of 4px or 8px where possible.
3.  **Line Length**: Ensure paragraph widths stay within the optimal 45-75 character range (use `max-width` with `ch` units).
4.  **Hierarchy**: Use weight (font-weight), color (muted vs. vibrant), and size to clearly distinguish between different levels of information.
5.  **Font Pairings**: Ensure font choices are cohesive. Favor modern, clean sans-serifs for a professional feel.

## Instructions
- Use relative units (`rem`, `em`, `ch`).
- Standardize body text to `1.5` or `1.6` line-height.
- Ensure all text meets accessibility contrast requirements.
- Update microcopy/labels during typesetting to ensure clarity.
